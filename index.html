IPhO Preparation
In the IPHO (International Physics Olympiad) preparation at the Physics Club, members engage in rigorous problem-solving sessions, advanced theoretical discussions, and practical experiments to enhance their physics knowledge and problem-solving skills. The focus is on challenging participants with complex physics concepts, promoting teamwork, and providing mock exams to simulate the IPHO environment and boost their chances of success in the prestigious competition.

Clash of Physicists
An intellectual showdown â€“ Clash of Physicists! Organized by the SXC Physics Club, this event will bring together the brightest minds from St. Xavier's College for a thrilling battle of physics knowledge.

Event Details:
Name: Clash of Physicists
Date: January 28, 2024
Time: 1:30 onwards
Venue: St. Xavier's College, Maitighar

Participation:
To ensure a fair, inclusive, and competitive environment, only six students from each section are invited to participate, making a total of 108 participants. These participants will be grouped into teams of four, with two students from Grade 11 and two from Grade 12.

Prizes:
The stakes are high, with the winning team set to receive a grand prize of Rs 5000, and the runner-up team not far behind with an award of Rs 3000. Both winning and runner-up teams will be presented with certificates and medals, recognizing their outstanding achievements.

Registration Detail:
Registration Fee: Rs 125 per person

Competition Format:
The Clash of Physicists will unfold in two rounds:

Objective Round
Participants will commence their Olympiad journey with a challenging qualifying round. This phase aims to evaluate their foundational understanding of physics concepts through a mix of multiple-choice questions (MCQ) and answer writing format. The questions will be shorter and relatively easy to attempt.

Subjective Round:
The grand finale will feature the top performers from the previous round competing for prestigious awards and accolades. The finale will celebrate academic excellence and recognize outstanding achievements. Participants will face high-ability subjective questions, and winners will be decided based on the quality of their answers.
